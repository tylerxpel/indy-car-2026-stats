Marketing Insights

What the data tells us about our audience
✓ Strength

Exceptional lead capture rate

96.1% of everyone who started the quiz became a lead — well above typical interactive content benchmarks. The quiz format is clearly working as a low-friction lead gen mechanism. Customers are engaged enough to follow through, likely because answering questions feels rewarding rather than transactional.

✓ Strength

Very low drop-off once started

91% completion rate from start to finish is strong. The 9-question structure and estimated 66-second completion time suggest the quiz length is well-calibrated — long enough to gather insight, short enough that people don't bounce. No major friction points in the question flow.

⚠ Opportunity

Low view-to-start rate (61.7%)

38% of viewers never clicked to start. Some of this is organic bounce or crawler traffic, but it also signals the intro screen or entry CTA may not be compelling enough. A stronger hook — clearer value prop, more personalized framing like "How well do you know XPEL?" — could meaningfully close this gap on future runs.

→ Audience Signal

Audience is overwhelmingly mobile — and iOS

85% of sessions were on mobile, with iOS at 61.4% of total and Android at 23.4%. This likely reflects the dealer/installer audience engaging from the shop floor or on-the-go. Any follow-up quiz, tool, or campaign targeting this audience should be designed iPhone-first with no desktop dependency.

⚠ Opportunity

Traffic concentrated in a 3-day window

317 of 415 views (76%) arrived June 19–21 — the likely send date of the campaign blast. Activity dropped sharply after June 22 with near-zero engagement. This quiz is generating essentially zero organic or long-tail traffic. Embedding the link in dealer portal nav, email footers, or regular nurture sequences would give it sustained shelf life.

→ Product Knowledge

Quiz results can map where education gaps exist

With 233+ completed sessions, there's a solid signal set from the Riddle answer-level data. Questions where wrong answers clustered should flag product lines or use cases we may be under-communicating in dealer training or sales materials. Cross-referencing those against the questions most closely tied to new products (PPF tiers, Ceramic Express) would be especially valuable.
